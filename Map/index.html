The Broad Street cholera outbreak was a severe outbreak of cholera that occurred in 1854 near Broad Street (now Broadwick Street) in Soho, London, England, and occurred during the 1846-1860 cholera pandemic happening worldwide. This outbreak, which killed 616 people, is best known for the physician John Snow's study of its causes and his hypothesis that germ-contaminated water was the source of cholera, rather than particles in the air

Some Important Terminology

Pandemic
a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease over a whole country or the world at a particular time
Endemic
a disease regularly occurring within an area or community.
Epidemic
a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time
